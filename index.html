Who We Are

About Oculis Iuris

Oculis Iuris — "In the Eyes of Law" — is the official Forensic Medicine club of JSS Medical College, Mysore. We are a community of curious medical minds united by a passion for forensic science, medico-legal principles, and the pursuit of justice through medicine.

We bridge the gap between clinical knowledge and legal application, equipping future doctors with the skills to navigate court testimonies, examine evidence, understand thanatology, and contribute meaningfully to the medico-legal system of India.

From hands-on case discussions to toxicology workshops and crime scene simulations, Oculis Iuris creates a space where forensic science comes alive.

Symbolism

The Logo & Its Meaning

Oculis Iuris · Est. JSS Medical College
01

The Eye of Horus

The central motif is the ancient Egyptian Eye of Horus — symbol of protection, royal power, and good health. In forensic context, it represents the all-seeing eye of justice that misses nothing, just as a forensic physician must observe with precision and objectivity.

02

The Scales of Justice

Embedded within the eye are the scales of Lady Justice — the universal symbol of balanced, impartial law. This reflects our commitment to truth in the medico-legal system, where a doctor's testimony can tip the scales between conviction and exoneration.

03

The Magnifying Glass

The magnifying glass overlaying the iris symbolizes investigation, meticulous examination, and forensic scrutiny. Every detail matters — from wound patterns to toxicology reports — and no evidence should go unexamined.

04

The Fingerprints

Flanking the seal are fingerprints — the oldest and most iconic tools of forensic identification. They represent individuality, identity, and the core principle of forensic medicine: every body tells a story.

05

Navy, Gold & White

Navy represents trust, authority, and the gravity of law. Gold symbolizes excellence, knowledge, and the pursuit of truth. White reflects clarity and the purity of scientific investigation — together they define our identity.

Monthly Feature

Case of the Month

🗓 March 2026

The Unidentified Male Found Near Railway Track — Suicidal or Homicidal?

ClassificationViolent Death
Difficulty★★★☆☆
CategoryTraumatology / Thanatology
Submitted ByEditorial Board, Oculis Iuris

A 35-year-old male was found dead near a railway track at 6 AM. The body showed multiple lacerating wounds over the lower extremities with near-complete amputation of the left leg. Rigor mortis was well-established and post-mortem lividity was fixed on the dorsal surface. No identity documents were found. Witnesses report hearing a "thud" around midnight.

On external examination: abrasions on palms, contused-lacerated wounds on face, and a faint ligature mark on the neck. Stomach was empty. Blood alcohol: negative. A crumpled note was found 4 metres away.

Question: Based on external examination findings, what is your primary impression? What specific features would you look for to distinguish ante-mortem from post-mortem railway injuries in this case?

📋 Discussion & Key Teaching Points

Primary impression: Suicidal railway death, pending full autopsy.

Ante-mortem indicators: Vital reaction (hemorrhage, swelling) around wounds indicates injuries occurred before death. Fixed lividity on dorsal surface is consistent with supine position after death — expected in a railway fatality. The ligature mark requires thorough examination; if antemortem, it suggests prior suicidal attempt or homicidal concealment.

Post-mortem railway injuries show no vital reaction, no hemorrhage tracking, and a pale, bloodless wound bed. The wheels cause crush-type amputations with typical "train wheel" contusion patterns.

Medico-legal significance: The note must be preserved as evidence. Scene examination, blood spatter pattern, and CCTV footage correlation are essential. Final determination of suicidal vs. homicidal vs. accidental requires complete autopsy, viscera preservation, and histopathology.
